About Me

Khoa is a senior at Cal studying Computer Science, and I enjoy writing bios that alternate between the first and third person. He is most interested in Natural Language Processing, Data Mining, and their applications in the real world. In my free time, I like to sail/kayak at the Berkeley Marina, box, travel and get lost in a new city, go hiking/rockclimbing, play tennis/pingpong, and bake cookies.

Khoa was a Distributed Computing/Data Science Intern at Autodesk in Summer 2014 working with the Design Graph team. He is currently an Undergraduate Student Instructor for EECS 70: Discrete Mathematics and Probability Theory under Professor Anant Sahai, where he teaches one recitation section of ~30 students and leads the development of the "virtual lab", a set of mini programming/simulation assignments in IPython designed to help students understand the course material better. Since January 2014, I have also been a research assistant working with Professor Heather Haveman and Sociology graduate student Cyrus Dioun on mining information from the Bitcoin and vintage wine markets.

Feel free to drop him a line at khoatran (at) berkeley (dot) edu and let's chat!
